The Ultimate Guide to Hosting a Summer Water Slide Party

July 12, 2026 | 5 min read

Nothing beats the heat like a massive water slide right in your backyard. But hosting a wet inflatable party requires a bit more planning than a standard dry bounce house. Here is our comprehensive guide.

1. Check Your Water Pressure & Hose Length

Our slides connect directly to your standard garden hose. However, you need to ensure your hose is long enough to reach from your spigot to the setup area. The hose must reach the back of the inflatable without stretching or pulling.

2. Plan for Drainage

Thousands of gallons of water will circulate during your party. Where will it go when the party is over? Setup areas should have a slight slope away from your home's foundation. Never set up a water slide in an area that already has poor drainage, or you'll end up with a mud pit.

"The difference between a good party and a great party is anticipating the logistics before the guests arrive."

3. Safety First

Wet vinyl is slippery outside of the designated slide areas. Ensure kids aren't running on the grass immediately after exiting the pool. Keep the blower motor and electrical cords completely elevated and away from pooling water.
